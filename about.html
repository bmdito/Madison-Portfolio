About Me

In my work, I aim to capture reality in its purest form through highly rendered, representational drawings. Historically, I used primarily oil and acrylic paint on canvas, but more recently have refined my practice to drawing mediums such as graphite and ballpoint pen. Most of my work is based on my own photographs. I work from pictures because I believe they are as close to reality as you can get. The translation process from photo to drawing creates an environment in which my viewers can see what I see â€“ or even bring to light parts of reality that are often overlooked. Recently I have begun to venture away from hyper-real art to explore a looser, abstracted style. For example, my series titled Coping With Change (2019) illustrates my personal difficulties struggling with change through a more metaphorical avenue of art.
